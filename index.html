Identifying the Problem

Brainstorming initial idea

Since all three of us has lived by ourselves, we agreed that cooking could be major problem to survie in college. Thus, we came up with this initial problem idea: ‘Undergraduate students who live off campus and live on their own do not have information to cook for themselves’

Refining initial idea

Ideation

Fourty Ideas

In order to expand my perspective on our information problem, I tried to come up with various medium which might solve the problem. Those ideas covered from a simple breakfast container to smart home appliance that helps users to get up on time

One Idea

Of many ideas, we had to chose only one to develop. Based on self critique and feedback from other peers, we decided to develop the idea of Coupon Alarm which was one of my 40 ideas. As we evaluate this idea, we concluded that an alarm would not solve the problem of having breakfast. Thus, we developed the coupon idea.

Prototype

Sketching prototype

For the first prototype, we just came up with unpolished ideas developed from my Coupon Alaram concept. Stacy sketched and created the prototype so that we can evaluate.

Refining prototype

In order to refine our prototype, we performed heauristic evalution and conducted user tests.

Final prototype

Based on feedback and research, we kept revised our prototype. After several revisions, we can finally created ideal prototype which is most likely to solve our design problem

We thought this prototype was ideal since its position on desgin dimension was where we expected it to be: fairly high usability with simple user interaction, time saving with Instant Order, and considerbly low cost with cheap coupons

Specification

Since specification was huge assignment, we split each section of the specification. Personally, I worked on problem statement and specification outline design. Based on feedback from the professor and self critique, we revised our specification draft for several times. For our final draft, we emphasized on "Show" before "Tell". We presented prototype pictures first and justified our rationale behind each design decision later.

Video Prototype

Decisions

Storyboard

After receiving a feedback that our problem presentation is confusing, we revised our first storyboard. We concluded to emphasize on "skipping breakfast" as major problem. Thus, I came up with new storyboard. These revised storyboard images are for the first half of the video, which is about daily morning routine of a typical, tired college student. We wanted to present that typical college student skips his/her breakfast due to not enough time to cook or buy. For the other half, we demonstrated major functions of our application, such as Instant Order.

Epilogue

We designed a breakfast coupon providing application called "APPetite" in order to solve the problem of skipping breakfast. Since we could not build actual application, we cannot tell this application will solve the problem or not. However, we can tell this application design is ideal since it is iterated for several times based on feedback and research. If "APPetite" is really built, we expect it to make college students have their breakfast so that their academic performance won't be affected. We hope them to 'survive college'.

Nothing was easy for each assignemnt. However, I could complete all tasks with the help from my teammates, Alyssa and Stacy. I believe our teamwork played important role to achieve the group assignments. During entire quarter, we had 5~6 face-to-face meetings and 2~3 online meetings. These meetings allowed to have better communication which led to better result. Moreover, this group project helped to find which role in a group fits me the most.

Flashing back entire design process, I think I could learn how to think as a designer. Designing consists of identifying the problem, ideating possible solution, creating prototype and testing. Each stage has repetition of critique and refinement before moving on the next stage. This valuable experience helped me to expand my design perspective.
